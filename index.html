The Best President Nigeria Never Had!

Chief Obafemi J. O. Awolowo (GCFR)

A Nigerian nationalist and statesman who played a key role in Nigeria's independence movement, the First and Second Republics and the Civil War.

Here's a time line of Chief Awolowo's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Ikenne, Ogun State.
  • 1920 - His education at this early stage was interrupted for several years by the sudden death of his father.
  • 1930s - He became an active member of the Lagos Youth Movement—later the Nigerian Youth Movement—and rose to become its secretary for the Western Province.
  • 1937 - Got married to Miss Hannah Idowu Dideolu Adelana.
  • 1944 - Went to London in 1944 to study law.
  • 1947 - Wrote the influential Path to Nigerian Freedom.
  • 1952 - He was the first Leader of Government Business and Minister of Local Government.
  • 1949 - He formed the cultural group known as “Egbe Omo Oduduwa.
  • 1951 - Form the political party, the Action Group (AG). His party won the first elections ever conducted in Western Nigeria.
  • 1952 - Became the Leader of Government Business and Minister for Local Government.
  • 1954 - Became the first Premier of the Western Region.
  • 1956 - His party won the elections again and also retained his position as Premier.
  • 1957 - His party was the first to move the motion for Nigeria's independence in the federal parliament and he obtained internal self-government for the Western Region.
  • 1959 - Became leader of the opposition in the federal House of Representatives, after a disappointing showing in the hard-fought 1959 elections and after the two other major parties had formed a coalition.
  • 1960 - He began to modify his earlier position, leaning toward socialism and advocating a neutral foreign policy rather than his earlier pro-Western position.
  • 1962 - With dissension growing in his own party over both ideology and administration, Awolowo fought to maintain ascendancy. Although he managed to prevail at the annual party conference.
  • 1963 - Following a trial for treasonable felony, he was jailed for 10 years in September 1963.
  • 1966 - He was released after a military coup took place that year.
  • 1968 - Awarded the Grand Band of the Order of the Star of Africa by the Republic of Liberia.
  • 1972 - Made Grand-Officer de l’Ordre National du Lion, Republique du Senegal.
  • 1979 - Nigerian presidential candidate.
  • 1983 - Nigerian presidential candidate.
  • 1987 - Died peacefully at his Ikenne home, at the age of 78.

Known by some as Awo and often referred to as the sage, but we know him as the Best President Nigeria Never Had!
